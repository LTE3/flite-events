## Design Context

### Users
**Primary**: 21-35 year olds in NYC looking for nightlife — club-goers, rave attendees, rooftop party seekers. They browse on their phones at 11pm deciding where to go tonight. Speed, trust, and vibe matter more than feature density.

**Secondary**: Event hosts and promoters — club owners, DJ collectives, party organizers who need to sell tickets, track door sales, and manage promoter commissions. They use the admin on laptops during the day.

**Job to be done**: Attendees want to find something worth going to tonight and buy tickets instantly with zero friction. Hosts want to sell out events and track every dollar. Promoters want to share links and see their commission stack up.

### Brand Personality
**Three words**: Raw. Nocturnal. Electric.

PulseTix is not a corporate ticketing platform. It's the friend who always knows where the party is. The tone is confident and direct — short copy, no fluff, no "Welcome to our platform!" energy. It speaks like a text from someone in the know.

**Emotional goals**: Excitement (something's happening tonight), exclusivity (you're in the loop), trust (your money and ticket are safe).

### Aesthetic Direction
**Visual tone**: Editorial nightlife — think a well-designed club flyer, not a SaaS dashboard. Dark because the context demands it (users are literally in the dark). High contrast. Confident typography. Photography-forward (real NYC nightlife images, not stock).

**Theme**: Dark. This is a nightlife product used at night. Dark mode is the only correct choice here.

**References**: Resident Advisor event pages (editorial tone), DICE app (clean dark ticketing), Boiler Room (raw energy), Supreme drops page (urgency + exclusivity).

**Anti-references**: Eventbrite (corporate, cluttered), generic SaaS landing pages, anything with purple-to-blue gradients, glassmorphism spam, or "AI-generated startup" aesthetics.

**Color**: Single accent — electric coral (#FF4D4D). Used sparingly for CTAs and emphasis. Neutrals tinted very slightly warm toward the brand hue. No secondary accent color. The accent works BECAUSE it's rare.

**Typography**: Bricolage Grotesque (display) — quirky ink traps, slightly irregular curves, feels like hand-set type on a club poster. Albert Sans (body) — geometric with humanist softness, excellent legibility on dark backgrounds at small sizes.

### Design Principles
1. **Nightlife-native**: Every decision should feel like it belongs at 1am. Dark surfaces, high contrast, no pastel softness.
2. **Photography over decoration**: Real images of real events carry the energy. Don't replace them with gradients, particles, or abstract patterns.
3. **Confident restraint**: One accent color. One display font. Generous whitespace. Let the content breathe instead of decorating every surface.
4. **Speed is the feature**: People decide in seconds. Big type, clear CTAs, instant feedback. No multi-step wizards when one click will do.
5. **Earn every element**: If it doesn't help the user find an event, buy a ticket, or manage their business — cut it.
